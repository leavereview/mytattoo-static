## Why Booking Systems Matter

### The Cost of Poor Booking

Without proper booking systems:
- No-shows eat into revenue and morale
- Double-bookings create client frustration
- Artists waste time on coordination
- Walk-ins disrupt scheduled work
- Deposits are inconsistently collected
- Communication falls through cracks

- Predictable schedules and income
- Reduced no-show rates
- Professional client experience
- Efficient use of artist time
- Clear communication
- Business data for decisions

## Booking Methods: Options and Trade-offs

### Phone/Text Booking

**How it works:** Clients call or text to book. You manage a calendar manually.

**Pros:**
- Personal touch
- Flexibility in conversation
- No technology barriers for clients
- Can screen bookings personally

**Cons:**
- Time-consuming for high volume
- Easy to miss messages
- No 24/7 availability
- Manual calendar management
- Deposit collection complicated

**Best for:** Very small operations, artists who value personal screening.

### Email Booking

**How it works:** Clients email to request appointments. You respond with availability.

**Pros:**
- Written record of communication
- Can review requests at convenient times
- Allows detailed design discussions

**Cons:**
- Slow back-and-forth to confirm
- Easy for messages to get buried
- Not real-time availability
- Manual calendar management

**Best for:** Custom work requiring significant pre-discussion.

### Social Media DMs

**How it works:** Clients book through Instagram or Facebook messages.

**Pros:**
- Where clients already are
- Can view their other tattoos/style
- Builds social media engagement

**Cons:**
- Unprofessional for business operations
- Messages easily lost
- No integration with calendar
- Difficult to manage at scale
- Platform changes affect your business

**Best for:** Initial contact only, not primary booking method.

### Online Booking Systems

**How it works:** Dedicated software shows real-time availability. Clients book directly, provide information, and pay deposits.

**Pros:**
- 24/7 booking availability
- Real-time calendar sync
- Automated reminders
- Integrated deposit collection
- Client information capture
- Reporting and analytics
- Professional appearance

**Cons:**
- Monthly software cost
- Learning curve
- Some clients prefer human contact
- Less screening of clients

**Best for:** Studios wanting efficiency and growth.

## Essential Booking Policies

### Deposit Policy

**Why deposits matter:**
- Reduce no-shows significantly
- Compensate for held time if cancelled
- Demonstrate client commitment
- Cover material costs for custom design

**Standard approaches:**
- Fixed amount ($50-$100 for smaller work)
- Percentage of estimated total (10-30%)
- Hourly minimum (first hour's rate)

**Deposit terms to define:**
- Refundability conditions
- Transfer to tattoo cost
- Forfeiture for no-show/late cancel
- Rescheduling allowances

### Cancellation Policy

**Typical terms:**
- 48-72 hours notice required for refund
- Less than 24 hours: deposit forfeited
- No-show: deposit forfeited
- One reschedule allowed without penalty

**Communication:**
- State policy clearly during booking
- Include in confirmation messages
- Remind in pre-appointment communication
- Enforce consistently

### Minimum Booking

**Options:**
- Minimum charge (e.g., $80 minimum)
- Minimum time (e.g., 1-hour minimum)
- Minimum deposit

**Rationale:** Small tattoos take setup time and supplies regardless of actual tattooing time.

### Walk-In Policy

**Approaches:**
- No walk-ins (appointment only)
- Walk-ins welcome based on availability
- Designated walk-in days/hours
- Flash-only walk-ins

**Considerations:**
- Artist preferences
- Studio traffic patterns
- Flash availability
- Scheduling flexibility

## The Booking Process

### Step 1: Initial Inquiry

**Information to gather:**
- Design concept/reference images
- Placement
- Approximate size
- Previous tattoo experience
- Any relevant health information

**How to gather:**
- Online booking form
- Email questionnaire
- Phone conversation
- Consultation appointment

### Step 2: Estimate and Confirmation

**Provide:**
- Estimated time required
- Price or price range
- Deposit amount
- Available appointment times
- What to bring/prepare

**Get confirmation:**
- Client accepts estimate
- Deposits paid
- Date/time confirmed

### Step 3: Pre-Appointment Communication

**Send:**
- Appointment confirmation
- Reminder (1-2 days before)
- Preparation instructions
- Studio address and parking info
- What to expect

**Timing:**
- Confirmation: Immediately after booking
- Reminder: 48 hours and/or 24 hours before

Automated reminders through [booking software](/tattoo-booking-software/) significantly reduce no-shows.

### Step 4: Check-In

**At arrival:**
- Verify identity
- Complete consent forms
- Review and approve design
- Confirm placement
- Process remaining payment or confirm method

### Step 5: Post-Appointment

**Follow up with:**
- Aftercare instructions (if not provided in person)
- Healing check-in (optional but valued)
- Touch-up policy reminder
- Review request
- Rebooking prompt (for ongoing work)

## Managing Different Appointment Types

### Standard Appointments

Straightforward booking for defined work:
- Clear time estimate
- Single session expected
- Standard pricing

### Custom Work Consultations

For complex or large pieces:
- Separate consultation appointment (sometimes paid)
- Design approval before tattoo appointment
- May require multiple sessions
- Deposit may be higher

### Multi-Session Projects

For sleeves, back pieces, etc.:
- Book first session initially
- Schedule subsequent sessions after each appointment
- Track progress and remaining work
- May offer package pricing

### Touch-Ups

For fixing or refreshing previous work:
- May be complimentary within timeframe (your work)
- May be charged for work by other artists
- Shorter appointments typically

### Cover-Ups

Complex assessment needed:
- Consultation often required
- Higher pricing typically
- Design limitations to communicate
- May need multiple sessions

## Reducing No-Shows

### Prevention Strategies

**Deposits:** The single most effective no-show reducer.

**Reminders:**
- Multiple touchpoints (confirmation, 48-hour, 24-hour)
- Text messages more effective than email alone
- Include cancellation policy reminder

**Confirmation requirement:**
- Ask clients to confirm 24-48 hours before
- No confirmation = appointment released

**Easy rescheduling:**
- Make rescheduling simple
- Clients more likely to reschedule than no-show if process is easy

### Handling No-Shows

**Immediate:**
- Document the no-show
- Forfeit deposit per policy
- Note in client record

**Follow-up:**
- Contact to understand reason (optional)
- Clear about rebooking terms
- Decide on future booking acceptance

**Policy considerations:**
- First offense vs repeat
- Circumstances (genuine emergency)
- Client value and history

### Tracking No-Show Rates

**Metrics to monitor:**
- Overall no-show percentage
- No-show by day of week
- No-show by artist
- No-show by appointment type

**Use data to:**
- Identify patterns
- Adjust policies
- Target reminders
- Evaluate changes

## Booking for Multiple Artists

### Centralised vs. Individual Booking

**Centralised:**
- One booking system for all artists
- Front desk or manager handles
- Consistent client experience
- Easier to manage availability

**Individual:**
- Each artist manages own bookings
- Different styles/preferences accommodated
- Artists have direct client relationships
- Can be inconsistent

**Hybrid:**
- Shared system with artist-specific settings
- Common policies, individual availability
- Often the best balance

### Managing Artist Schedules

**Considerations:**
- Varying work days/hours
- Different appointment lengths
- Guest artist scheduling
- Time off and vacations
- Chair rental arrangements
